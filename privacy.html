Privacy Policy

Last updated: 24 April 2026 · Effective: 01 May 2026

The short version: We collect what we need to run the scheduling service, we don't sell your data, and you can delete your account at any time.

1. Information we collect

Information you provide

  • Account data: name, email, password hash, profile photo.
  • Booking data: attendee name, email, phone number, notes, meeting topic.
  • Calendar integration: read/write access to connected Google or Microsoft calendars, limited to the scope you authorise.
  • Support messages: any content you send via our contact or abuse-reporting forms.

Information collected automatically

  • Device & usage: browser type, IP address, referring URL, pages viewed, timestamps.
  • Cookies: session cookies for sign-in; a single first-party cookie for your preferred timezone.

2. How we use your information

PurposeLegal basis (GDPR)
Operating the booking service and sending calendar invitesPerformance of a contract
Sending transactional emails (confirmations, reminders)Performance of a contract
Preventing fraud and abuseLegitimate interest
Product analytics (aggregated, no profiling)Legitimate interest
Marketing emails (opt-in only)Consent

3. Who we share it with

We share your data only with processors strictly required to run the service:

  • Hosting: cloud infrastructure in the EU (Frankfurt region).
  • Email delivery: transactional email provider.
  • Calendar sync: Google or Microsoft, limited to the scopes you authorise.

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not use it to train advertising models.

4. International transfers

Primary servers are located in the European Union. Where data is transferred outside the EEA, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission.

5. Data retention

  • Active account data: retained while your account is active.
  • Deleted accounts: erased within 30 days, except where we are legally required to retain records (e.g. invoicing, 10 years).
  • Server logs: 14 days.

6. Your rights

Subject to applicable law, you can:

  • access a copy of your personal data;
  • correct or update inaccurate data;
  • delete your data ("right to be forgotten");
  • port your data to another service;
  • object to processing based on legitimate interest;
  • withdraw consent for marketing at any time.

To exercise any of these, email privacy@meet.example or use the contact form.

7. Children

meet is not directed to children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 16.

8. Security

We protect data in transit with TLS 1.3 and at rest with AES-256. Access is restricted, audited, and logged. No system is perfectly secure, however, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Changes

When we make material changes we will notify you by email or a banner on the service at least 30 days before they take effect.
